# 02 - Research Template

> **Status:** Approved
>
> **Version:** 1.0
>
> **Last Updated:** July 2026

---

# Purpose

Provide a standardized framework for conducting and documenting research at Garza Intelligence Labs.

Every research project should follow this structure to ensure consistency, repeatability, transparency, and operational value.

The objective is not simply to record information.

The objective is to generate operational intelligence.

---

# Research Title

Clearly identify the subject being researched.

---

# Research Objective

Why is this research being conducted?

What problem is it intended to solve?

How will this information improve Garza Intelligence Labs, its products, or its customers?

---

# Operational Importance

Explain why this research matters.

Research should always connect back to one or more of the following:

- Organization
- Operations
- Optimization

If the research does not improve at least one of these areas, reconsider whether it should be performed.

---

# Research Questions

List the primary questions this research intends to answer.

Examples:

- What do we currently know?
- What don't we know?
- What assumptions require validation?
- What operational decisions depend on this information?

Research begins with questions.

---

# Existing Knowledge

Summarize what is already known before beginning research.

Include:

Established facts.

Current understanding.

Previous research.

Relevant historical context.

---

# Sources

Document every source consulted.

For each source include:

- Source name
- Author or organization
- Publication date
- Date accessed
- Reliability Tier
- Relevant notes

---

# Findings

Document objective findings only.

Separate facts from interpretation.

Avoid speculation.

Use evidence whenever possible.

---

# Pattern Analysis

Identify relationships.

Identify trends.

Identify operational patterns.

Ask:

Why does this happen?

When does it happen?

What influences it?

---

# Operational Intelligence

Translate findings into actionable intelligence.

Examples:

How should this influence product design?

How should this influence recommendations?

How should this improve operational decision making?

Research should always produce practical value.

---

# Confidence Assessment

Evaluate the reliability of the research.

Confidence Level:

Confirmed

High

Moderate

Low

Unknown

Explain why the assigned confidence level is appropriate.

---

# Limitations

Document what remains uncertain.

Examples:

Missing data.

Conflicting information.

Limited sample size.

Changing operational conditions.

Research should openly acknowledge uncertainty.

---

# Unknown Questions

Every completed research project should generate additional questions.

Examples:

What remains unanswered?

What deserves future investigation?

What assumptions still require validation?

Research should continuously expand.

---

# Recommended Next Research

Identify logical follow-up projects.

Research should naturally connect to future work.

Knowledge should compound over time.

---

# Product Opportunities

Document ideas generated during research.

These may include:

New features.

Operational improvements.

Engineering ideas.

User experience improvements.

Future products.

Ideas should be captured even if they are not immediately actionable.

---

# Triple O Evaluation

Explain how this research contributes to:

## Organization

How does it improve the organization of knowledge?

---

## Operations

How does it improve operational understanding?

---

## Optimization

How does it improve future decision making?

---

# Executive Summary

Provide a concise summary of:

Key findings.

Operational implications.

Recommended actions.

Future priorities.

A reader should understand the value of the research without reading the full document.

---

## Notes

This template establishes the standard structure for all Garza Intelligence Labs research projects.

Every completed research document should strengthen the Garza Intelligence Labs Knowledge Base and contribute to future operational intelligence.
