# Purpose

Define the standardized research methodology of Garza Intelligence Labs.

Research exists to transform information into operational intelligence.

The objective is not to collect facts.

The objective is to understand systems.

Every research effort conducted by Garza Intelligence Labs should follow this methodology regardless of industry or product.

---

# Philosophy

Research is the foundation of intelligent decision making.

Knowledge without organization creates noise.

Knowledge without context creates confusion.

Knowledge without application creates waste.

Research only becomes valuable when it improves decisions.

---

# Mission

Every research project should:

- Organize information.

- Understand relationships.

- Discover patterns.

- Identify opportunities.

- Generate operational intelligence.

## Every completed research project should improve a product, a decision, or the understanding of a system.

---

# Research Principles

Every research effort should be:

Objective.

Evidence-based.

Repeatable.

Transparent.

Actionable.

Continuously improving.

---

# The GIL Research Cycle

Every research project follows six phases.

## Phase One — Define

Clearly identify the problem.

Define why the research matters.

Determine what decisions the research will support.

---

## Phase Two — Discover

Gather information from credible sources.

Collect multiple perspectives.

Avoid early conclusions.

Prioritize understanding before analysis.

---

## Phase Three — Organize

Structure information into logical categories.

Identify relationships.

Remove duplication.

Separate facts from assumptions.

---

## Phase Four — Analyze

Identify patterns.

Identify operational implications.

Question assumptions.

Compare conflicting evidence.

Determine confidence.

---

## Phase Five — Apply

Translate findings into operational intelligence.

Every research project should answer:

How does this improve our products?

How does this improve our decisions?

How does this improve our customers' experience?

---

## Phase Six — Expand

Every completed research project should generate new research questions.

Research should continuously compound.

Learning never concludes.

---

# Research Questions

Every project should begin with questions.

Examples include:

What problem are we trying to understand?

Why does this matter?

What decisions depend on this research?

What information already exists?

What information is missing?

What assumptions require verification?

---

# Evidence Standards

Conclusions should be supported by multiple independent sources whenever possible.

Conflicting information should be documented rather than ignored.

Assumptions should always be labeled as assumptions.

Confidence should increase through verification.

---

# Operational Intelligence

Research is complete only when it creates operational value.

Every project should conclude with:

Key findings.

Operational implications.

Product opportunities.

Future research questions.

Recommended actions.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Research should never be considered finished.

As new information becomes available, previous conclusions should be re-evaluated.

The objective is continuous improvement—not permanent certainty.

---

# Success Criteria

Research succeeds when:

It improves understanding.

It improves decision making.

It improves products.

It improves customer outcomes.

Every completed project should strengthen the knowledge base of Garza Intelligence Labs.

---

# Final Principle

Garza Intelligence Labs does not research to accumulate information.

We research to reduce uncertainty.

Every question answered should make better decisions possible.

Every answer should generate new questions.

Every insight should move us closer to building products that organize, operate, and optimize the world around us.

---

## Notes

This document establishes the official research methodology of Garza Intelligence Labs.

All future research projects should follow this methodology regardless of product, industry, or domain.
