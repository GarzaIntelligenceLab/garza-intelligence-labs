> **Status:** Approved
>

---

# Purpose

Establish the long-term research priorities of Garza Intelligence Labs.

The Research Roadmap serves as the master planning document for every research initiative conducted by the organization.

Rather than collecting information randomly, Garza Intelligence Labs will pursue research according to operational impact, strategic value, and alignment with the Triple O Philosophy.

Research should always be intentional.

---

# Philosophy

Research is not a checklist.

It is a continuous process of reducing uncertainty.

Every completed research project should improve our understanding of a system, reveal new opportunities, and generate additional research questions.

The roadmap should evolve alongside the organization.

---

# Mission

Guide the growth of the Garza Intelligence Labs Knowledge Base.

Prioritize research.

Identify knowledge gaps.

Organize future investigations.

Ensure research efforts produce measurable value.

---

# Research Prioritization

Every research initiative should receive a priority level.

## Critical

Required before product release.

Research that directly impacts core functionality, product accuracy, operational intelligence, or customer trust.

---

## High

Provides significant competitive advantage.

Research that meaningfully improves recommendations, personalization, prediction, or user experience.

---

## Medium

Improves product quality and operational understanding.

Useful, but not required for initial implementation.

---

## Low

Interesting or beneficial.

May improve future versions but does not significantly affect current priorities.

---

## Future

Research intended for long-term development.

Important ideas that should not distract from current objectives.

---

# Research Categories

The Knowledge Base should continuously expand through multiple research domains.

## Product Research

Understanding products, workflows, user experience, and operational systems.

---

## User Research

Understanding customer behavior, psychology, expectations, frustrations, and decision making.

---

## Operational Research

Understanding how systems function in real-world environments.

---

## Historical Research

Understanding how systems have evolved over time.

Historical intelligence often explains present operational behavior.

---

## Technical Research

Understanding engineering approaches, emerging technologies, implementation methods, and software architecture.

---

## Competitive Research

Understanding existing products, market gaps, strengths, weaknesses, and opportunities.

Research competitors to understand the landscape.

Never to imitate it.

---

## Future Research

Exploring emerging technologies, industries, methodologies, and opportunities that may shape future Garza Intelligence Labs products.

↓

↓

↓

↓

↓

---

# Research Review

Every completed research project should be reviewed for:

Accuracy.

Source quality.

Operational value.

Product implications.

Future opportunities.

No research should enter the Knowledge Base without review.

---

# Success Metrics

Research should be evaluated by:

Questions answered.

Knowledge gaps identified.

Operational improvements generated.

Product enhancements inspired.

Future research opportunities discovered.

Research quality is measured by impact—not quantity.

---

# Living Roadmap

The Research Roadmap is a living document.

Projects should move between priority levels as new information becomes available.

Completed research should generate new initiatives.

The roadmap should never become static.

---

# Triple O Alignment

Every research initiative should strengthen at least one element of the Triple O Philosophy.

## Organization

Does this improve how knowledge is organized?

---

## Operations

Does this improve understanding of how systems function?

---

## Optimization

Does this improve future decision making or system performance?

Research that does not strengthen at least one of these areas should be reconsidered.

---

# Final Principle

Research is not performed to accumulate knowledge.

Research is performed to reduce uncertainty.

Every completed research project should make Garza Intelligence Labs more capable of building intelligent systems that organize information, improve operations, and optimize the way people experience the world around them.
