> **Status:** Approved
>
> **Version:** 1.0

> **Last Updated:** July 2026

---

# Purpose

Define the official Intelligence Mission classifications used by Garza Intelligence Labs.

Mission Classification provides a standardized method for organizing, prioritizing, assigning, and evaluating Operational Intelligence Missions.

Every Intelligence Mission should receive a classification before research begins.

Mission Classification ensures that Operational Intelligence is developed intentionally and aligned with organizational priorities.

---

# Philosophy

Not every question deserves the same level of attention.

Some missions answer immediate operational problems.

Others explore future opportunities.

Mission Classification allows Garza Intelligence Labs to allocate Operational Intelligence Navigators toward the work that creates the greatest long-term value.

Classification creates focus.

Focus creates understanding.

Understanding expands human capability.

---

# Mission Categories

Operational Intelligence Missions are divided into the following classifications.

---

# OIR-E

## Exploratory Intelligence

Purpose:

Discover unknown opportunities, relationships, or areas of future research.

Questions often begin with:

What exists?

What are we missing?

What should we investigate?

Examples:

- Understanding Disney Cruise operations

- Exploring emerging AI technologies

- Investigating new tourism trends

Exploratory Missions create future intelligence.

---

# OIR-V

## Validation Intelligence

Purpose:

Verify existing research.

Confirm accuracy.

Update outdated information.

Validation Missions protect the integrity of the Living Knowledge Graph.

Examples:

- Confirming attraction operating procedures

- Updating restaurant information

- Verifying transportation changes

Intelligence should remain current.

---

# OIR-C

## Competitive Intelligence

Purpose:

Study competitors.

Identify strengths.

Identify weaknesses.

Discover opportunities.

Examples:

- TouringPlans

- Disney Tourist Blog

- Thrill Data

- Standby Skipper

- Universal Orlando

Competition reveals opportunities.

---

# OIR-U

## User Intelligence

Purpose:

Understand human behavior.

Study emotions.

Observe decision making.

Identify operational friction.

Examples:

Guest psychology.

Family decision making.

Vacation planning.

Stress points.

Operational confusion.

People remain the center of Operational Intelligence.

---

# OIR-O

## Operational Intelligence

Purpose:

Understand how systems function.

Identify inefficiencies.

Study operational relationships.

Examples:

Transportation.

Resort operations.

Queue management.

Crowd movement.

Dining operations.

Operations determine experience.

---

# OIR-P

## Product Intelligence

Purpose:

Improve existing products.

Generate new features.

Discover opportunities for Unified Intelligence Platforms.

Examples:

Recommendation improvements.

GeoHUD™ enhancements.

Conversation Engine improvements.

Adaptive Guidance improvements.

Research should improve products.

---

# OIR-S

## Strategic Intelligence

Purpose:

Support long-term organizational decisions.

Examples:

New industries.

Potential partnerships.

Business expansion.

Future Unified Intelligence Platforms.

Strategic planning.

Strategic Intelligence guides the future of Garza Intelligence Labs.

---

# Mission Priority

Every Intelligence Mission should receive a priority level.

Priority One

Critical

Immediate organizational value.

---

Priority Two

High

Important to current initiatives.

---

Priority Three

Standard

Regular research.

---

Priority Four

Future

Important, but not time sensitive.

---

Priority Five

Exploratory

Interesting opportunities that may become valuable later.

---

# Mission Scope

Every Intelligence Mission should define its expected scope.

Small

Hours.

---

Medium

Days.

---

Large

Weeks.

---

Expedition

Months.

Expeditions should only be assigned when organizational value justifies the effort.

---

# Multiple Classifications

Some Intelligence Missions naturally span multiple categories.

Example:

Disney Transportation Study

Classification:

Operational Intelligence

User Intelligence

Product Intelligence

Multiple classifications provide a more accurate understanding of the mission.

# Final Principle

Every Intelligence Mission should begin with purpose.

Mission Classification ensures that Operational Intelligence Navigators pursue the right questions at the right time for the right reasons.

Well-classified missions create better intelligence.

Better intelligence creates better products.
