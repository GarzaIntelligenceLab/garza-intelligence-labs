# Purpose

The first week of an Operational Intelligence Navigator is designed to establish the foundation for every future contribution to Garza Intelligence Labs.

During your first week, your objective is not speed.

Your objective is understanding.

You are not expected to know everything.

You are expected to learn how Garza Intelligence Labs thinks.

---

# Week One Mission

By the end of your first week, you should understand:

• The mission of Garza Intelligence Labs

• The Triple O Philosophy

• Complex Human Systems

• Operational Intelligence

• The Living Knowledge Graph

• The responsibilities of an Operational Intelligence Navigator

• How research becomes products

Everything you learn during your first week will prepare you for your first Intelligence Mission.

---

# Day One

Your first responsibility is understanding the organization.

Read:

- README.md
- ARCHITECTURE.md

Then continue with:

- GIL Canon
- Research Standards
- Core Technologies (Overview)

Your objective is not memorization.

Your objective is perspective.

Understand why Garza Intelligence Labs exists before learning how it operates.

---

# Day Two

Study the Research Division.

Understand:

Research Standards.

Source Reliability.

Research Template.

Research Roadmap.

Disney Research Roadmap.

Learn how Operational Intelligence is developed through disciplined research.

---

# Day Three

Study the Living Knowledge Graph.

Learn:

How information becomes knowledge.

How knowledge becomes operational intelligence.

How intelligence improves products.

Your responsibility is not collecting information.

Your responsibility is improving understanding.

---

# Day Four

Observe existing Intelligence Missions.

Review completed projects.

Study:

Research methodology.

Evidence quality.

Knowledge Graph updates.

Product recommendations.

Engineering opportunities.

Learn from previous Navigators before beginning your own work.

---

# Day Five

Receive your first Intelligence Mission.

Your first mission is intentionally limited in scope.

It is designed to evaluate:

Research discipline.

Critical thinking.

Source evaluation.

Documentation quality.

Attention to detail.

No one is expected to be perfect.

Everyone is expected to improve.

---

# Expectations

During your first week:

Ask questions.

Remain curious.

Challenge assumptions.

Support conclusions with evidence.

Respect existing research.

Document everything.

Operational Intelligence grows through disciplined thinking.

---

# Success

Your first week is successful when you can confidently explain:

What Garza Intelligence Labs is.

Why Operational Intelligence matters.

How the Living Knowledge Graph functions.

How research improves products.

How an Operational Intelligence Navigator contributes to the organization.

Understanding comes before contribution.

---

# Looking Forward

Completing your first week marks the beginning of your journey as an Operational Intelligence Navigator.

From this point forward, every Intelligence Mission you complete will strengthen the Living Knowledge Graph, improve Operational Intelligence, and contribute to technologies that expand human capability.

The journey has only begun.

---

## Final Principle

> **Before you can navigate intelligence, you must first understand the organization that creates it.**
