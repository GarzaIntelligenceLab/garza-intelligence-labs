## Founder's Letter
When I was sixteen years old, I started my first job at a bank. One of my earliest responsibilities was manually sorting loan files, account records, and paperwork. At the time, I didn't have a background in operations or technology. I just remember thinking that there had to be a better way to organize the work.
A short time later, our bank acquired another institution. Their records filled rows of filing cabinets throughout the office, and I was responsible for organizing those documents into our electronic filing system. Most people looked at the project and saw paperwork. I saw something different. I saw disconnected information, inefficient workflows, and time being lost because systems weren't designed to work together.
I didn't realize it then, but those experiences shaped the way I would look at problems for the rest of my life.
Over the years, I found myself asking the same question in completely different situations. Whether I was improving banking operations, organizing data, planning a Disney vacation, or evaluating new technology, I kept asking myself why people accepted unnecessary complexity as normal. Why should information live in five different places? Why should people waste valuable time navigating systems that create more work instead of less?
Those questions became the foundation of Garza Intelligence Labs.
This company wasn't created because I wanted to build software. It was created because I believe people deserve better systems. Technology should organize information, simplify operations, and help people make better decisions—not create more confusion. The goal has never been to replace people or remove the human element from work. The goal is to eliminate unnecessary friction so people can spend their time where it creates the most value.
Everything we build is guided by three principles: Organization. Operations. Optimization. These principles remind us that every product should bring clarity to complexity, improve the way work flows, and leave people more confident than they were before they used it.
I don't believe Garza Intelligence Labs will be successful because of the technology we use. Technology will continue to change, just as it always has. I believe we will be successful if we stay committed to one simple idea: make complexity manageable.
If this letter is being read years after it was written, I hope that principle still guides every decision we make. Products will change. Industries will change. Technology will change. Our responsibility will not. We exist to help people spend less time fighting systems and more time solving the problems that matter.
Welcome to Garza Intelligence Labs.
— Austin Garza
