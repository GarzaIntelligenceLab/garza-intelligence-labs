# Purpose

This document establishes the official research submission procedure for all Operational Intelligence Navigators (OINs).

Research is not considered complete when writing ends.

An Intelligence Mission is complete only when it has been properly submitted, reviewed, approved, and integrated into the Organizational Intelligence of Garza Intelligence Labs.

Proper submission protects the quality, consistency, and long-term value of every Intelligence Mission.

---

# Before You Submit

Before submitting your Intelligence Mission, confirm that you have completed the following.

☐ Operational Intelligence Brief (2,500–3,000 words unless otherwise directed)

☐ Intelligence Summary

☐ Knowledge Graph Contributions

☐ Product Opportunities

☐ Future Intelligence Missions

☐ GIL Citation Standard

☐ Self-review completed

☐ All supporting documents attached

Do not submit incomplete work.

---

# Submission Package

Every Intelligence Mission should be submitted as a complete Mission Package.

Your submission should include:

• Operational Intelligence Brief (.md or approved format)

• Supporting research notes

• Source documentation

• Knowledge Graph relationships

• Product opportunities

• Future mission recommendations

Additional supporting materials such as spreadsheets, images, diagrams, or datasets may be included when appropriate.

---

# Naming Convention

Use the following naming format.

Mission Number

↓

Mission Title

↓

Navigator Designation

Example:

OIR-002

Walt Disney World Transportation Systems

OIN Overcash

Consistency allows Organizational Intelligence to remain organized as the repository grows.

---

# Founder Submission

Submit the completed Mission Package to the Founder for initial review.

Founder Review focuses on:

• Mission objectives

• Organizational value

• Operational Intelligence

• Triple O

• Living Knowledge Graph contributions

• DOIE improvement

The Founder may approve the mission, request revisions, or initiate a Rescue Mission.

---

# Revision Process

Revisions are a normal part of Operational Intelligence.

A request for revision does not indicate failure.

Instead, revisions strengthen Organizational Intelligence before implementation.

If revisions are requested:

Review all feedback carefully.

Address every concern.

Document significant changes.

Resubmit the updated Mission Package.

Operational Intelligence improves through refinement.

---

# Rescue Missions

If an Intelligence Mission cannot achieve its objectives, the mission may transition into Rescue Mission status.

During a Rescue Mission:

Useful intelligence is preserved.

Knowledge Graph relationships are retained.

Lessons learned are documented.

A future Operational Intelligence Navigator may continue the investigation.

No meaningful intelligence should be discarded.

---

# Mission Approval

An Intelligence Mission is officially complete only after receiving Founder approval.

Mission approval confirms that:

✓ Organizational Intelligence has improved.

✓ The Living Knowledge Graph should be updated.

✓ DOIE benefits from the findings.

✓ Future opportunities have been documented.

Mission completion is determined by organizational improvement—not submission.

---

# After Approval

Following approval:

The mission receives LOCKED status.

Mission numbers are permanently retired.

Historical records remain unchanged.

Future discoveries should generate new Intelligence Missions rather than modifying approved historical work.

Organizational Intelligence grows through addition.

---

# Mission Feedback

Following mission completion, every Operational Intelligence Navigator is encouraged to reflect on the mission.

Consider:

What worked well?

What proved difficult?

What would improve future Intelligence Missions?

What documentation should be improved?

Your feedback helps strengthen the Operational Intelligence Navigator Program for future Navigators.

---

# Final Reminder

Operational Intelligence is not measured by the number of missions submitted.

It is measured by the capability those missions create.

Submit work you are proud to place into the permanent history of Garza Intelligence Labs.

Every approved Intelligence Mission becomes part of the institutional memory of GIL.

Protect that responsibility.
