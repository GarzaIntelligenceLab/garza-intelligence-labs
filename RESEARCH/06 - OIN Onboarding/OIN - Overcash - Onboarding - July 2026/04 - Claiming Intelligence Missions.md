# 04 - Claiming Intelligence Missions

> **Status:** Approved
>
> **Version:** 1.0
>
> **Last Updated:** July 2026

---

# Purpose

Define the process for claiming, conducting, and completing Intelligence Missions within Garza Intelligence Labs.

Every Intelligence Mission exists to answer meaningful questions, strengthen the Living Knowledge Graph, and improve Operational Intelligence.

Once claimed, a Navigator becomes responsible for the successful completion of that mission.

---

# Philosophy

Intelligence Missions are not assignments.

They are opportunities to expand the understanding of Garza Intelligence Labs.

Every mission should leave the organization more intelligent than before.

Mission ownership creates accountability.

Accountability creates quality.

---

# Mission Lifecycle

Every Intelligence Mission follows the same lifecycle.

Mission Created

↓

Mission Reviewed

↓

Mission Claimed

↓

Research Conducted

↓

Intelligence Report Submitted

↓

Peer Review

↓

Knowledge Graph Updated

↓

Mission Complete

---

# Mission Identification

Every mission receives a unique identifier.

Example:

```
OIR-001
Disney Guest Psychology

OIR-002
Transportation Operations

OIR-003
Magic Kingdom Queue Behavior
```

Mission identifiers should never be reused.

They become part of the permanent research history of Garza Intelligence Labs.

---

# Claiming a Mission

Before beginning research, an Operational Intelligence Navigator should:

Review the mission objective.

Understand the expected deliverables.

Review previous related research.

Identify potential sources.

Confirm the mission has not already been claimed.

Once claimed, the Navigator assumes responsibility for the mission until completion or reassignment.

---

# Mission Ownership

Only one Primary Navigator should own an Intelligence Mission.

Additional Navigators may contribute when approved by leadership.

The Primary Navigator is responsible for:

Research quality.

Source integrity.

Documentation.

Mission progress.

Final Intelligence Report.

Knowledge Graph recommendations.

Ownership ensures accountability.

---

# Conducting the Mission

Every Intelligence Mission should follow GIL Research Standards.

Research should be:

Evidence-based.

Objective.

Well documented.

Repeatable.

Operationally meaningful.

Quality should always take priority over speed.

---

# Progress Updates

Large Intelligence Missions should provide regular progress updates.

Updates should include:

Current status.

Major findings.

Challenges encountered.

Additional resources required.

Potential changes in scope.

Communication prevents duplicate work and improves collaboration.

---

# Mission Completion

An Intelligence Mission is complete when:

The research objective has been satisfied.

Evidence has been verified.

The Intelligence Report has been submitted.

Knowledge Graph recommendations have been documented.

Product opportunities have been identified.

Future research questions have been recorded.

Completion is measured by organizational value—not page count.

---

# Reassignment

If a Navigator is unable to complete a mission, leadership may reassign ownership.

Research should never become abandoned.

Incomplete work should always be preserved for future Navigators.

Institutional knowledge is never discarded.

---

# Mission Priorities

Not every Intelligence Mission has equal importance.

Leadership may assign priority levels based on organizational needs.

Suggested priorities:

Priority One — Critical

Priority Two — High

Priority Three — Standard

Priority Four — Long-Term

Priority Five — Exploratory

Priority reflects organizational impact—not difficulty.

---

# Collaboration

Operational Intelligence is a team effort.

Navigators are encouraged to:

Share resources.

Recommend sources.

Discuss ideas.

Challenge assumptions.

Support fellow Navigators.

Every contribution strengthens the organization.

---

# Final Principle

Claim every Intelligence Mission with curiosity.

Conduct every mission with discipline.

Complete every mission with integrity.

Leave the Living Knowledge Graph stronger than you found it.

Mission complete is not the end.

It is the beginning of greater Operational Intelligence.
