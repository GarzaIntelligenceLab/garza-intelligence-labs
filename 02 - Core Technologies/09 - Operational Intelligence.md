# Purpose

Define the Recommendation Engine, a core technology of Garza Intelligence Labs responsible for generating intelligent, explainable, and adaptive recommendations within Complex Human Systems.

The Recommendation Engine transforms operational understanding into actionable guidance.

Its objective is not to make decisions for people.

Its objective is to help people make better decisions.

---

# Definition

The Recommendation Engine continuously evaluates the current operational state of a system and recommends actions that best align with an individual's goals, preferences, constraints, and changing circumstances.

Recommendations should adapt as the system evolves.

---

# Philosophy

A recommendation should never exist without reasoning.

Every recommendation represents an informed hypothesis built upon operational intelligence.

Recommendations should increase confidence—not remove choice.

---

# Mission

Understand.

Evaluate.

Recommend.

Explain.

Adapt.

Improve.

Every recommendation should strengthen human capability.

---

# Core Principles

The Recommendation Engine should always be:

Context-aware.

Adaptive.

Explainable.

Evidence-based.

Goal-oriented.

Continuously learning.

Human-centered.

---

# Recommendation Process

Every recommendation follows the same process.

Understand the individual.

↓

Understand the system.

↓

Identify constraints.

↓

Evaluate available options.

↓

Predict outcomes.

↓

Generate recommendations.

↓

Measure results.

↓

Improve future recommendations.

---

# Inputs

The Recommendation Engine evaluates information from multiple sources.

Examples include:

Living Knowledge Graph.

Conversation Engine.

Operational Intelligence.

Historical patterns.

Current operational conditions.

User goals.

Environmental changes.

Time.

Recommendations should never rely upon a single source of information.

---

# Recommendation Criteria

Every recommendation should consider:

Individual objectives.

Operational efficiency.

Timing.

Risk.

Confidence.

Environmental conditions.

Available alternatives.

Expected outcomes.

Recommendations should balance multiple competing factors.

---

# Explainability

Every recommendation should answer:

Why is this being recommended?

What information influenced this recommendation?

What alternatives were considered?

How confident is this recommendation?

Explainability builds trust.

---

# Adaptation

Recommendations should continuously adapt when:

Operational conditions change.

User priorities change.

New information becomes available.

Unexpected events occur.

The best recommendation five minutes ago may no longer be the best recommendation now.

---

# Human Authority

Recommendations support decision making.

They do not replace it.

The individual always retains final authority.

Operational intelligence informs.

People decide.

# Success Criteria

The Recommendation Engine succeeds when:

Recommendations become increasingly accurate.

Users develop confidence.

Decision fatigue decreases.

Operational outcomes improve.

Human capability increases.

Trust grows naturally.

---

# Future Vision

Future Recommendation Engines should become increasingly personalized while remaining transparent and explainable.

As understanding improves, recommendations should require fewer assumptions and greater confidence.

Every recommendation should represent the best available operational understanding at that moment.

---

# Final Principle

The purpose of a recommendation is not to tell people what to do.

The purpose is to illuminate the path most likely to help them achieve what matters most.
