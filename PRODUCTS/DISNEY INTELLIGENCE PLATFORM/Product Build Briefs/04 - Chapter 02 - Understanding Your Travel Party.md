# 04 - Chapter 02 - Understanding Your Travel Party

> **Status:** Approved
>
> **Version:** 1.0
>
> **Last Updated:** July 2026

---

# Purpose

Define the engineering, design, and interaction requirements for Chapter Two of the Disney Intelligence Platform.

Chapter Two exists to understand the people the vacation must serve.

The platform should begin learning about relationships, personalities, preferences, physical needs, and travel dynamics before asking logistical questions.

Every recommendation made later in the experience depends upon understanding the people first.

---

# Chapter Objective

Answer one question:

> "Who is this vacation really for?"

Disney vacations are rarely designed around one individual.

The platform should identify the unique needs of the entire travel party before recommending experiences.

The guest should feel that every person traveling matters.

---

# User Outcome

By the end of Chapter Two the guest should feel:

- Understood
- Comfortable
- Relieved
- Optimistic
- Confident the platform understands their family

The guest should never feel categorized.

---

# Platform Outcome

By the end of Chapter Two the platform should understand:

- Number of travelers
- Family relationships
- Ages
- General mobility
- Energy levels
- Special accommodations
- Sensory considerations
- Celebration events
- Planning personalities
- Initial travel dynamics

The platform should gather enough information to personalize future conversations without overwhelming the guest.

---

# Conversation Philosophy

The platform should remain conversational.

Never interrogational.

Questions should feel like curiosity rather than data collection.

The platform should demonstrate active listening throughout the chapter.

---

# Primary Conversation

The conversation should naturally progress through topics such as:

Who is coming with you?

Who is most excited?

Is anyone celebrating something special?

Are there young children?

Teenagers?

Grandparents?

Does anyone have mobility needs?

Sensory sensitivities?

Dietary restrictions?

Different energy levels?

The conversation should adapt naturally based upon previous answers.

---

# Listening Signals

The platform should quietly begin recognizing patterns without labeling the guest.

Examples include:

- Relaxation seeking
- Attraction focused
- Food motivated
- Celebration focused
- Child centered
- Adult centered
- Luxury preference
- Budget awareness
- Adventure seeking
- Flexible planner
- Detailed planner

These are internal signals.

The guest should not feel placed into predefined categories.

---

# Adaptive Conversation

Future questions should change depending upon previous answers.

Examples:

A family with toddlers should receive different follow-up questions than two adults celebrating an anniversary.

A guest traveling alone should experience a different conversation than a multigenerational family.

The platform should avoid asking unnecessary questions.

Understanding should always feel personalized.

---

# Emotional Design

This chapter should reinforce one feeling:

"This vacation was designed for us."

The platform should communicate empathy without sounding artificial.

It should acknowledge concerns naturally.

Every completed answer should increase confidence.

---

# Visual Design

Continue the warm earth-tone visual language established in Chapter One.

The interface should feel welcoming.

Illustrations should communicate family, exploration, and togetherness.

Avoid stock-photo aesthetics.

The visual identity should remain premium, calm, and timeless.

---

# Interaction Design

Every interaction should encourage conversation.

Large touch targets.

Comfortable spacing.

Subtle haptic feedback.

Smooth page transitions.

The guest should never feel rushed.

Progress should feel meaningful.

---

# Engineering Requirements

The platform should:

Support dynamic question paths.

Save progress continuously.

Allow guests to return later.

Adapt future questions based upon previous answers.

Remember travel party information throughout the entire vacation.

Accessibility should remain a first-class requirement.

---

# Success Criteria

Chapter Two succeeds when:

The platform understands the travel party without making the guest feel interviewed.

Future personalization becomes possible.

The guest believes the platform genuinely understands their family.

The conversation remains enjoyable.

---

# Version 1 Scope

Chapter Two should focus exclusively on understanding the people traveling.

Do not introduce:

- Budgets
- Resorts
- Transportation
- Dining strategy
- Park itineraries
- Route optimization
- Wait times
- Operational planning

Those belong in later chapters.

Maintain discipline.

---

# Future Opportunities

Potential future enhancements include:

- Returning traveler recognition.
- Family profiles.
- Child preference memory.
- Celebration history.
- Health and accessibility profiles.
- Vacation personality evolution across multiple trips.

These ideas should not delay Version 1.

---

# Final Principle

Every Disney vacation is unique because every family is unique.

The Disney Intelligence Platform should never assume that two vacations are the same simply because two families visit the same parks.

Before the platform can understand the vacation...

It must first understand the people experiencing it.
