# 08 - Chapter 06 - Budget Philosophy

> **Status:** Approved
>
> **Version:** 1.0
>
> **Last Updated:** July 2026

---

# Purpose

Define the engineering, design, and interaction requirements for Chapter Six of the Disney Intelligence Platform.

Chapter Six exists to understand how guests prefer to spend money during their Disney vacation.

The objective is not to determine how much money they have.

The objective is to understand what they believe is worth spending money on.

Every recommendation should respect the guest's financial philosophy.

---

# Chapter Objective

Answer one question:

> "What is worth spending money on?"

This chapter identifies financial priorities before introducing budgets or recommendations.

---

# User Outcome

By the end of Chapter Six the guest should feel:

- Understood
- Comfortable
- Never judged
- Confident that recommendations will respect their priorities

The platform should never make guests feel wealthy or limited.

---

# Platform Outcome

By the end of Chapter Six the platform should understand:

- Spending philosophy
- Savings philosophy
- Value perception
- Dining priorities
- Resort priorities
- Merchandise interest
- Experience priorities
- Convenience preferences
- Budget flexibility
- Purchase confidence

---

# Conversation Philosophy

The platform should never ask:

"What is your budget?"

Instead, it should discover how the guest naturally approaches spending.

Money is personal.

The conversation should remain respectful and judgment-free.

---

# Primary Conversation

Topics may include:

What experiences are worth spending extra money on?

Where do you usually prefer to save money while traveling?

Would you rather stay at a nicer resort or experience more activities?

Would you rather purchase souvenirs or enjoy additional dining experiences?

How important is convenience?

Would you spend more to reduce stress?

If something would make your trip unforgettable, would you consider spending a little more?

---

# Adaptive Learning

The platform should begin estimating:

- Value sensitivity
- Experience priorities
- Convenience weighting
- Spending flexibility
- Purchase confidence

These should remain flexible and continue evolving.

---

# Emotional Design

Money should never create stress.

Guests should feel that the platform is helping them maximize value rather than maximize spending.

---

# Visual Design

Continue the established visual language.

Warm.

Comfortable.

Premium.

No charts.

No spreadsheets.

No financial dashboard appearance.

---

# Interaction Design

The conversation should remain natural.

Avoid percentages.

Avoid financial terminology.

Avoid forcing numerical answers whenever possible.

The guest should feel like they are discussing priorities—not finances.

---

# Engineering Requirements

The platform should:

Adapt follow-up questions dynamically.

Avoid repetitive financial questions.

Continuously update spending philosophy.

Support future refinement during the vacation.

Maintain accessibility across all supported devices.

---

# Success Criteria

Chapter Six succeeds when:

The platform understands what the guest values financially.

Future recommendations respect spending philosophy.

The guest never feels judged by the conversation.

---

# Version 1 Scope

Chapter Six should focus exclusively on financial philosophy.

Do not introduce:

- Actual budgets
- Cost calculations
- Resort recommendations
- Dining recommendations
- Itinerary generation
- Discounts
- Purchasing decisions

Those belong in later chapters.

---

# Future Opportunities

Future versions may incorporate:

- Real-time vacation spending
- Budget forecasting
- Dining optimization
- Merchandise tracking
- Vacation cost summaries
- Savings recommendations

These features should support—not replace—the guest's financial preferences.

---

# Final Principle

Money should never determine whether a Disney vacation feels magical.

The Disney Intelligence Platform should help guests spend intentionally, confidently, and in alignment with what matters most to them.
