# 07 - Chapter 05 - Travel Style

> **Status:** Approved
>
> **Version:** 1.0
>
> **Last Updated:** July 2026

---

# Purpose

Define the engineering, design, and interaction requirements for Chapter Five of the Disney Intelligence Platform.

Chapter Five exists to understand how the guest prefers to travel throughout their Disney vacation.

The objective is not to determine what the guest wants to experience, but how they prefer to experience it.

Every itinerary should reflect the guest's natural rhythm.

---

# Chapter Objective

Answer one question:

> "How do you like to travel?"

This chapter establishes the pace of the vacation before operational planning begins.

---

# User Outcome

By the end of Chapter Five the guest should feel:

- Comfortable
- Understood
- Confident
- Excited

The guest should recognize that the platform is adapting to their lifestyle rather than forcing one upon them.

---

# Platform Outcome

By the end of Chapter Five the platform should understand:

- Morning preferences
- Evening preferences
- Rope drop interest
- Midday break habits
- Resort break habits
- Transportation preferences
- Walking tolerance
- Rest frequency
- Pool day interest
- Park hopping interest
- Vacation pace

---

# Conversation Philosophy

Questions should feel natural.

The platform should avoid asking guests to choose between "good" and "bad" planning habits.

Instead, it should discover how they naturally enjoy vacationing.

---

# Primary Conversation

Topics may include:

How do you usually like to start your mornings?

Would you rather rope drop or enjoy a slower morning?

How do you feel about afternoon breaks?

Do you enjoy returning to your resort during the day?

How much walking feels comfortable?

Would you rather accomplish more or move at a relaxed pace?

Do you enjoy staying until park close?

How important are pool days?

Do you enjoy resort hopping?

How flexible do you like your schedule?

---

# Adaptive Learning

The platform should begin estimating:

- Daily energy profile
- Walking capability
- Rest requirements
- Transportation efficiency
- Park endurance
- Vacation rhythm

These estimates should continue improving throughout the trip.

---

# Emotional Design

Guests should begin imagining themselves living their vacation.

Planning should begin feeling personal.

The platform should communicate that there is no "correct" Disney pace.

Only the pace that best fits the guest.

---

# Visual Design

Maintain the established design language.

Warm colors.

Premium simplicity.

Natural movement.

Minimal distractions.

Every screen should reinforce calm confidence.

---

# Interaction Design

Conversation should remain uninterrupted.

Large touch targets.

Comfortable spacing.

Subtle haptic feedback.

Smooth page transitions.

Progress should feel meaningful.

---

# Engineering Requirements

The platform should:

Adapt questions dynamically.

Learn from previous answers.

Avoid unnecessary follow-up questions.

Update the guest profile continuously.

Support future behavioral learning.

Maintain accessibility across all supported devices.

---

# Success Criteria

Chapter Five succeeds when:

The platform understands how the guest naturally prefers to move throughout a Disney vacation.

Future itinerary generation reflects the guest's preferred pace rather than generic optimization.

The guest feels the platform is designing a vacation specifically for them.

---

# Version 1 Scope

Chapter Five should focus exclusively on travel style.

Do not introduce:

- Budget planning
- Resort recommendations
- Park recommendations
- Dining plans
- Daily itineraries
- Route optimization
- Adaptive Decision Architecture

Those belong in later chapters.

---

# Future Opportunities

Future versions may adapt travel style automatically using:

- Walking history
- Attraction completion rates
- Real-time fatigue
- Weather
- Park congestion
- Guest behavior throughout the vacation

The platform should become increasingly accurate with every completed trip.

---

# Final Principle

The best Disney vacation is not the busiest.

It is the one experienced at the pace that feels most natural to the guest.

The Disney Intelligence Platform should adapt to the guest.

The guest should never feel pressured to adapt to the platform.
