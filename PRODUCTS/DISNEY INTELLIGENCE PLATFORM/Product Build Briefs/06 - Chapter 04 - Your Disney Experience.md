# 06 - Chapter 04 - Your Disney Experience

> **Status:** Approved
>
> **Version:** 1.0
>
> **Last Updated:** July 2026

---

# Purpose

Define the engineering, design, and interaction requirements for Chapter Four of the Disney Intelligence Platform.

Chapter Four exists to understand how the guest naturally experiences Disney.

Two guests may visit the same parks, stay at the same resort, and have identical budgets, yet desire completely different vacations.

This chapter identifies those behavioral differences before operational planning begins.

The platform should begin understanding the guest's Disney personality without assigning labels.

---

# Chapter Objective

Answer one question:

> "How do you naturally experience Disney?"

This chapter should discover habits rather than preferences.

The goal is to understand how the guest enjoys moving through Disney—not simply what attractions they enjoy.

---

# User Outcome

By the end of Chapter Four the guest should feel:

- Understood
- Excited
- Curious
- Recognized

Guests should begin realizing the platform understands how they enjoy Disney rather than how Disney is traditionally planned.

---

# Platform Outcome

By the end of Chapter Four the platform should understand:

- Disney experience level
- Vacation traditions
- Park habits
- Planning confidence
- Exploration style
- Attraction philosophy
- Resort philosophy
- Entertainment preferences
- Shopping habits
- Pace of travel

The platform should quietly build a behavioral model that influences every future recommendation.

---

# Conversation Philosophy

This chapter should feel nostalgic.

Guests should naturally begin reflecting on previous Disney memories or imagining future ones.

The platform should encourage storytelling rather than checklist completion.

The conversation should remain relaxed and conversational.

---

# Primary Conversation

Topics may include:

Is this your first Disney vacation?

Have you visited before?

Do you usually rope drop or sleep in?

Do you enjoy staying until park close?

Do you enjoy exploring resorts?

Do you like discovering hidden details?

Do you prefer having a plan or wandering?

Are fireworks a must?

Do you enjoy festivals?

Do you spend more time shopping or riding attractions?

Would you rather accomplish everything or enjoy whatever the day brings?

Conversation paths should adjust naturally based upon previous answers.

---

# Behavioral Signals

Without informing the guest, the platform should begin recognizing patterns such as:

- Explorer
- Completionist
- Atmosphere Seeker
- Food Explorer
- Thrill Rider
- Entertainment Focused
- Luxury Traveler
- Efficient Planner
- Flexible Traveler
- First-Time Guest
- Disney Veteran

These are internal operational signals.

They are never presented as labels.

---

# Adaptive Conversation

Every conversation should adapt.

Examples:

A first-time visitor should receive different questions than a Disney Vacation Club member.

A guest who enjoys resort hopping should experience different follow-up questions than a guest who spends every minute inside the parks.

Guests should never answer irrelevant questions.

---

# Emotional Design

This chapter should create anticipation.

Guests should begin imagining themselves walking through Disney.

The platform should reinforce the idea that no two Disney vacations are identical.

---

# Visual Design

Continue the established visual language.

Warm earth tones.

Natural textures.

Magical accent colors.

Illustrations should evoke exploration, discovery, and nostalgia.

Avoid stereotypical travel imagery.

---

# Interaction Design

Interactions should encourage reflection.

Animations should remain subtle.

Haptic feedback should reinforce meaningful selections.

Transitions should feel like turning pages in a storybook rather than navigating software.

---

# Engineering Requirements

The platform should:

Adapt questions dynamically.

Remember behavioral patterns.

Avoid duplicate questioning.

Update the guest profile continuously.

Maintain accessibility across all devices.

Support future learning as additional trips are completed.

---

# Success Criteria

Chapter Four succeeds when:

The platform understands how the guest naturally enjoys Disney.

Behavioral patterns become clear without requiring explicit labels.

The guest feels the conversation is becoming increasingly personalized.

Future operational planning becomes significantly more accurate.

---

# Version 1 Scope

Chapter Four should focus exclusively on behavioral understanding.

Do not introduce:

- Resort recommendations
- Daily itineraries
- Budgets
- Transportation planning
- Dining strategy
- Route optimization
- Vacation Book generation

Those belong in later chapters.

Maintain focus.

---

# Future Opportunities

Future versions may recognize returning guests and identify changes in travel behavior over time.

The platform may eventually learn:

- Changing interests
- New traditions
- Children's changing ages
- Favorite experiences
- Annual vacation patterns

Behavior should evolve alongside the family.

---

# Final Principle

Disney is experienced differently by every guest.

The purpose of this chapter is not to discover the "best" way to experience Disney.

It is to discover the guest's way.

Only then can the Disney Intelligence Platform begin building an experience that truly feels personal.
