# 05 - Chapter 03 - What Matters Most

> **Status:** Approved
>
> **Version:** 1.0
>
> **Last Updated:** July 2026

---

# Purpose

Define the engineering, design, and interaction requirements for Chapter Three of the Disney Intelligence Platform.

This chapter exists to identify what the guest values most during their Disney vacation.

Every future recommendation should prioritize what matters most—not simply what is most popular.

The goal is not to rank Disney experiences.

The goal is to understand how this family defines a successful vacation.

---

# Chapter Objective

Answer one question:

> "What kind of Disney experience are you hoping to create?"

Every family measures success differently.

The platform should discover those priorities before building the Vacation Book.

---

# User Outcome

By the end of Chapter Three the guest should feel:

- Excited
- Inspired
- Understood
- Curious about what comes next

The guest should begin imagining their vacation.

Planning should start feeling real.

---

# Platform Outcome

By the end of Chapter Three the platform should understand relative priorities such as:

- Attractions
- Food
- Entertainment
- Characters
- Relaxation
- Resorts
- Shopping
- Budget
- Convenience
- Exploration
- Hidden experiences
- Spontaneity

The platform should understand priorities without forcing rigid rankings.

---

# Conversation Philosophy

The platform should never ask:

"What do you like?"

Instead, it should ask questions that reveal values naturally.

Examples:

What would make you leave Disney saying, "That was worth every penny?"

If you could only remember one part of this vacation ten years from now, what would you hope it is?

Would you rather experience more attractions or spend more time enjoying the atmosphere?

Would you rather discover hidden Disney details or experience every major attraction?

The guest should feel like they are describing a dream—not filling out a survey.

---

# Adaptive Priorities

The platform should quietly begin building weighted priorities.

Not labels.

Signals.

Every answer should influence future recommendations.

Priorities should evolve throughout onboarding.

Nothing should be permanently locked.

---

# Emotional Design

This chapter should increase anticipation.

The guest should begin visualizing their vacation.

Every completed answer should make Disney feel closer.

---

# Engineering Requirements

The platform should:

Support conversational branching.

Continuously update guest priorities.

Allow guests to revise previous answers.

Preserve context from Chapters One and Two.

Avoid repetitive questioning.

---

# Success Criteria

Chapter Three succeeds when:

The platform understands what success looks like for this family.

Guests begin emotionally investing in the experience.

Future recommendations become meaningfully personalized.

---

# Version 1 Scope

Do not recommend:

- Specific attractions
- Restaurants
- Resorts
- Budgets
- Daily itineraries

This chapter exists only to understand priorities.

Recommendations begin later.

---

# Future Opportunities

Future versions may identify evolving priorities during the vacation and adapt recommendations accordingly.

The platform should recognize that what mattered before arrival may differ from what matters on Day Four.

---

# Final Principle

Disney does not create the perfect vacation.

Families do.

The role of the Disney Intelligence Platform is to understand what matters most, then organize, operate, and optimize around those priorities.
