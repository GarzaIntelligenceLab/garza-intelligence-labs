# 09 - Chapter 07 - Resort Intelligence

> **Status:** Approved
>
> **Version:** 1.0
>
> **Last Updated:** July 2026

---

# Purpose

Define the engineering, design, and interaction requirements for Chapter Seven of the Disney Intelligence Platform.

Chapter Seven exists to identify the resort experience that best supports the guest's vacation goals.

The platform should recommend resorts based on how guests want to experience Disney—not simply by price or popularity.

Every resort recommendation should have clear reasoning.

---

# Chapter Objective

Answer one question:

> "Where should your Disney vacation begin and end each day?"

The resort is more than a hotel.

It becomes the operational headquarters of the vacation.

---

# User Outcome

By the end of Chapter Seven the guest should feel:

- Confident
- Informed
- Excited
- Understood

The guest should understand why certain resorts better support their vacation than others.

---

# Platform Outcome

By the end of Chapter Seven the platform should understand:

- Desired transportation methods
- Resort atmosphere
- Deluxe vs Moderate vs Value preferences
- Disney Vacation Club interest
- Space requirements
- Walking preferences
- Skyliner interest
- Monorail interest
- Dining priorities
- Budget alignment

The platform should begin narrowing the best operational choices.

---

# Conversation Philosophy

The conversation should not begin with:

"What resort do you want?"

Instead, it should discover:

"How do you want your vacation to feel when you're not inside the parks?"

Recommendations should naturally emerge from previous chapters.

---

# Primary Conversation

Topics may include:

How important is transportation?

Would you rather walk, ride the Skyliner, Monorail, boat, or bus?

How important is having space to relax?

Do you enjoy spending time at your resort?

Would you rather save money on your room or enhance your resort experience?

Do you enjoy resort hopping?

How important are dining options at your resort?

How much time do you expect to spend outside the parks?

---

# Resort Intelligence

Recommendations should consider:

- Transportation efficiency
- Family size
- Vacation pace
- Budget philosophy
- Resort amenities
- Dining
- Walking distance
- Operational convenience
- Long-term value

Popularity should never outweigh compatibility.

---

# Recommendation Philosophy

Every recommendation should explain:

Why it fits.

Why alternatives ranked lower.

What tradeoffs exist.

What type of vacation it supports.

Guests should understand the reasoning—not simply the ranking.

---

# Emotional Design

Guests should begin picturing themselves arriving at their resort.

This chapter should increase anticipation while reducing uncertainty.

---

# Visual Design

Use immersive imagery.

Warm colors.

Natural textures.

Premium presentation.

Recommendations should feel curated rather than generated.

---

# Interaction Design

Guests should compare resorts naturally.

Comparisons should focus on experience—not specifications.

The interface should encourage exploration rather than decision pressure.

---

# Engineering Requirements

The platform should:

Generate personalized resort rankings.

Explain recommendation reasoning.

Support comparison mode.

Adapt recommendations as new information becomes available.

Remember resort preferences throughout the vacation planning process.

---

# Success Criteria

Chapter Seven succeeds when:

Guests understand which resorts best support their vacation.

Recommendations feel personalized.

The guest gains confidence rather than additional choices.

---

# Version 1 Scope

Chapter Seven should introduce:

- Personalized resort recommendations
- Operational reasoning
- Resort comparisons

Do not generate:

- Daily itineraries
- Route optimization
- Attraction planning
- Vacation Book

Those belong in later chapters.

---

# Future Opportunities

Future versions may include:

- Live resort availability
- DVC point optimization
- Rental comparison
- Room recommendation engine
- Preferred room location recommendations
- Seasonal resort analysis

---

# Final Principle

The best Disney resort is not the most expensive.

It is the one that best supports the vacation the guest is trying to create.

Every recommendation should prioritize compatibility over popularity.
