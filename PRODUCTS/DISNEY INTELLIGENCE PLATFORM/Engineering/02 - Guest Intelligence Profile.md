# 02 - Guest Intelligence Profile

> **Status:** Approved
>
> **Version:** 1.0
>
> **Last Updated:** July 2026

---

# Purpose

Define the complete digital representation of every guest within the Disney Intelligence Platform.

The Guest Intelligence Profile (GIP) serves as the platform's continuously evolving understanding of the guest.

Rather than storing isolated pieces of information, the Guest Intelligence Profile organizes every meaningful insight into a living operational profile that improves throughout the entire vacation lifecycle.

The Guest Intelligence Profile is the foundation upon which every recommendation, prediction, and adaptive decision is built.

---

# Vision

The Disney Intelligence Platform should never ask the guest the same question twice unless circumstances have changed.

Every meaningful interaction should increase the platform's understanding.

Every completed vacation should make future vacations more personalized.

The Guest Intelligence Profile exists to remember.

---

# Philosophy

People evolve.

Families evolve.

Disney evolves.

The Guest Intelligence Profile should evolve alongside them.

It should never become static.

---

# Core Principles

The Guest Intelligence Profile should be:

Living.

Adaptive.

Transparent.

Explainable.

Privacy-conscious.

Continuously improving.

---

# Profile Architecture

The profile is composed of multiple intelligence layers.

Each layer contributes to the Dynamic Operational Intelligence Engine.

---

# Identity Layer

Stores permanent information about the guest.

Examples include:

- Guest name
- Household members
- Family relationships
- Ages
- Accessibility needs
- Celebration history
- Disney Vacation Club ownership
- Annual Pass status
- Home airport
- Home region

This layer changes infrequently.

---

# Vacation Philosophy Layer

Defines how the guest prefers to experience Disney.

Examples include:

- Vacation goals
- Emotional priorities
- Pace of travel
- Preferred planning style
- Budget philosophy
- Resort philosophy
- Attraction philosophy
- Dining philosophy
- Entertainment philosophy

This layer guides every recommendation.

---

# Behavioral Intelligence Layer

Continuously learns how the guest actually behaves.

Examples include:

- Walking pace
- Attraction completion rate
- Dining habits
- Shopping behavior
- Resort usage
- Transportation preferences
- Rest frequency
- Notification engagement
- Decision patterns

Behavior should always outweigh assumptions.

---

# Operational Layer

Represents the current vacation.

Examples include:

- Active reservations
- Resort
- Park reservations
- Dining reservations
- Daily plans
- Current location
- Walking distance
- Time ahead or behind schedule
- Current weather context
- Operational disruptions

This layer changes continuously.

---

# Preference Layer

Stores evolving guest preferences.

Examples include:

- Favorite attractions
- Favorite restaurants
- Favorite resorts
- Favorite snacks
- Preferred transportation
- Preferred room locations
- Preferred park order
- Preferred experiences

Preferences should never be assumed permanent.

---

# Learning Layer

The platform should continuously record:

Accepted recommendations.

Rejected recommendations.

Manual itinerary changes.

Completed experiences.

Skipped experiences.

End-of-trip reflections.

Guest feedback.

Every completed vacation should improve future recommendations.

---

# Confidence Layer

Every major profile attribute should maintain a confidence score.

Examples include:

Dining Preference

High Confidence

Walking Pace

Medium Confidence

Entertainment Preference

Low Confidence

The platform should acknowledge uncertainty internally.

Higher confidence produces stronger recommendations.

---

# Intelligence Relationships

Every profile element should influence other elements.

Examples:

Young children influence:

- Walking pace
- Park duration
- Dining timing
- Resort recommendations

Budget philosophy influences:

- Dining
- Resorts
- Merchandise
- Transportation

The profile should function as an interconnected intelligence network.

Not isolated fields.

---

# Profile Evolution

The Guest Intelligence Profile should continuously improve through:

Conversation.

Behavior.

Operational events.

Vacation history.

Guest corrections.

Changing family dynamics.

The platform should become increasingly accurate over time.

---

# Engineering Requirements

The Guest Intelligence Profile should:

Support continuous updates.

Support version history.

Never overwrite historical vacations.

Track profile evolution.

Support future machine learning.

Maintain explainability.

Gracefully recover from incomplete information.

Protect guest privacy.

---

# Success Criteria

The Guest Intelligence Profile succeeds when:

The platform rarely asks unnecessary questions.

Recommendations become increasingly personalized.

Guests feel understood without repeating themselves.

Each completed vacation improves the next one.

---

# Future Opportunities

Future versions may include:

Cross-vacation trend analysis.

Predictive family behavior.

Life-stage adaptation.

Child growth awareness.

Long-term Disney tradition recognition.

Multi-generation family intelligence.

Personalized celebration history.

---

# Final Principle

The Guest Intelligence Profile is not a database.

It is the platform's evolving understanding of the people it serves.

Every recommendation should begin here.

Every adaptive decision should be informed by it.

Every completed vacation should make it wiser.

---

## Notes

The Guest Intelligence Profile is the primary intelligence source for the Dynamic Operational Intelligence Engine.

Every component of the Disney Intelligence Platform should either contribute to or benefit from the Guest Intelligence Profile.
