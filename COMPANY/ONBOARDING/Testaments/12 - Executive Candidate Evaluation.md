# Testament 11 — Executive Candidate Evaluation

> **Status:** Approved
>
> **Version:** 1.0
>
> **Last Updated:** July 2026

---

## Purpose

Establish the executive evaluation process used to identify leaders capable of protecting the philosophy, culture, and long-term vision of Garza Intelligence Labs.

This evaluation exists to understand how a candidate thinks, reasons, learns, and leads. It is not intended to measure personality, but rather the candidate's ability to strengthen the Canon while remaining faithful to the principles of Garza Intelligence Labs.

---

# Executive Philosophy

Garza Intelligence Labs does not select executives solely because of experience, technical ability, or credentials.

Executives are entrusted with protecting the philosophy of the organization.

Knowledge can be taught.

Technical skills can be learned.

Character, curiosity, judgment, and operational thinking must already exist.

Executives should challenge ideas—not the mission.

---

# Evaluation Principles

Every executive candidate should demonstrate:

- Intellectual Curiosity
- Operational Thinking
- Sound Judgment
- Humility
- Adaptability
- Integrity
- Respect for Human-Centered Technology
- Alignment with the Triple O Philosophy

---

# Section I — Mission & Purpose

### 1.

Why do you want to help build Garza Intelligence Labs instead of joining an established organization?

### 2.

What kind of legacy do you hope your work leaves behind?

### 3.

What does **capability** mean to you?

---

# Section II — Operational Intelligence

### 4.

Describe a workflow or process you have personally improved.

How did you identify the problem?

How did you measure success?

### 5.

Tell us about a time you challenged an existing standard.

Why did you challenge it?

Would you make the same decision today?

---

# Section III — Decision Making

### 6.

Describe a difficult decision you made that produced a poor outcome.

What did you learn?

### 7.

How do you determine whether a decision is supported by evidence rather than assumption?

---

# Section IV — Logic Assessment

### Scenario

Two departments are blaming each other for declining performance.

Department A believes Department B is creating delays.

Department B believes Department A is providing poor information.

You have limited evidence, executive pressure to act quickly, and one week before leadership expects a recommendation.

Walk us through your reasoning.

Do not tell us your conclusion.

Explain the process you would use to arrive at one.

---

# Section V — Pressure Assessment

### Scenario

Six months after launch, one of Garza Intelligence Labs' flagship products receives significant public criticism.

Engineering requests additional time.

Marketing wants to defend the product publicly.

Customers are frustrated.

Investors are demanding answers.

You have one hour before meeting with the executive team.

What do you do first?

Why?

---

# Section VI — Philosophy

### 8.

After reading the GIL Canon, which philosopher do you believe most closely aligns with the philosophy of Garza Intelligence Labs?

Explain your reasoning.

### 9.

Which philosopher do you believe aligns the least with the Triple O Philosophy?

Explain why.

### 10.

How would you improve the Triple O Philosophy using the ideas of the philosopher you selected without compromising its core principles?

---

# Section VII — Reflection

### 11.

What have you changed your mind about during the past year?

What caused that change?

### 12.

Which principle within the GIL Canon do you disagree with the most?

Why?

If you were responsible for improving it, what would you change?

---

# Final Question

Why should Garza Intelligence Labs trust you with helping shape its future?

---

# Evaluation Standard

Candidates are not evaluated based upon agreement.

Candidates are evaluated by:

- Quality of reasoning
- Intellectual honesty
- Curiosity
- Operational thinking
- Leadership under uncertainty
- Ability to improve systems
- Respect for evidence
- Alignment with the philosophy of Garza Intelligence Labs

---

# Final Principle

The purpose of this evaluation is not to find people who think exactly like us.

It is to find people capable of strengthening Garza Intelligence Labs while remaining faithful to its mission, vision, philosophy, and people.

The strongest candidates will challenge ideas with evidence, improve systems with humility, and protect the philosophy that defines the organization.
