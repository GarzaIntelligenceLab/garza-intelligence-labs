# Purpose

Define the Living Knowledge Graph, the foundational intelligence model used by Garza Intelligence Labs to understand, represent, and continuously improve Complex Human Systems.

The Living Knowledge Graph is not a database.

It is a continuously evolving network of relationships that models how people, environments, operations, information, and time interact within a system.

Every Unified Intelligence Platform developed by Garza Intelligence Labs is built upon a Living Knowledge Graph.

---

# Definition

A Living Knowledge Graph is an evolving representation of a Complex Human System.

Rather than storing isolated information, the graph continuously maps relationships between entities, events, behaviors, environments, operations, and decisions.

As new intelligence becomes available, the graph grows, adapts, and refines its understanding of the system.

Knowledge is never considered complete.

---

# Philosophy

Knowledge is not the collection of facts.

Knowledge is the understanding of relationships.

The Living Knowledge Graph exists to answer not only:

"What is?"

But also:

Why?

How?

When?

Where?

Who?

And what changes when something else changes?

---

# Mission

Organize knowledge.

Understand relationships.

Preserve history.

Learn continuously.

Support intelligent decision making.

Every addition to the graph should improve the system's understanding of the world it represents.

---

# Core Principles

The Living Knowledge Graph should always be:

Living.

Connected.

Explainable.

Expandable.

Historically aware.

Continuously improving.

Evidence-based.

Operationally meaningful.

---

# Intelligence Over Information

Traditional systems store information.

The Living Knowledge Graph stores understanding.

Information tells us:

A ride is closed.

Understanding tells us:

How that closure affects transportation, nearby attractions, dining demand, crowd movement, guest behavior, and future recommendations.

Relationships create intelligence.

---

# Core Components

Every Living Knowledge Graph contains interconnected nodes.

Examples include:

People.

Places.

Processes.

Events.

Objects.

Operations.

Time.

Behavior.

Rules.

Resources.

Relationships.

Every node exists within a larger operational system.

---

# Relationships

Relationships are the foundation of intelligence.

Examples include:

A guest influences an itinerary.

Weather influences transportation.

Transportation influences arrival times.

Arrival times influence wait times.

Wait times influence dining behavior.

Dining behavior influences walking patterns.

No event exists independently.

---

# Time

Time is a first-class component of the graph.

The graph should understand:

Past.

Present.

Predicted future.

Historical context should never be discarded.

Understanding how a system changes over time is essential to understanding the system itself.

---

# Continuous Learning

The Living Knowledge Graph continuously evolves through:

Research.

Operational events.

User interactions.

Behavioral observations.

Historical analysis.

Verified external information.

Every interaction should improve understanding.

---

# Explainability

Every conclusion produced by the graph should be explainable.

The graph should always be capable of answering:

What information contributed to this conclusion?

What relationships were evaluated?

How confident is this conclusion?

Intelligence without explanation should not be trusted.
